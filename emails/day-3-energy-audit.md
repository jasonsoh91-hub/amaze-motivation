Hey [First Name],

Quick question:

When was the last time you finished a day and thought, "Wow, I have so much energy right now"?

If you're like most people, your answer is somewhere between "last year" and "I don't remember."

Here's the thing—**you don't have an energy problem. You have a leak problem.**

---

**TODAY'S EXERCISE: The Energy Audit**

Grab that journal again. Draw two columns:

**WHAT GIVES ME ENERGY** | **WHAT DRAINS ME**

Under each column, list at least 10 things.

Don't overthink this. Go with your gut.

Some examples to get you started:

**Energy Givers:**
- Deep conversations with people who get it
- Exercise that doesn't feel like punishment
- Creating something with my hands
- Solitude before 9am
- Learning something new

**Energy Drainers:**
- Commuting
- Unresolved conflict
- Saying yes when I mean no
- Scrolling social media without intention
- Meetings that should have been emails

---

**THE TRUTH ABOUT "BUSY"**

Being busy doesn't drain you.

Doing work that doesn't align with who you are drains you.

Spending time with people who don't see you drains you.

Ignoring what you actually want drains you.

You're not tired from effort. You're tired from pretending.
