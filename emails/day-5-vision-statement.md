Subject: Day 5: Who do you want to become? (not "what do you want to achieve")

Hey [First Name],

Quick question:

When you imagine your future self—who are you picturing?

I don't mean "what job do you have" or "what's in your bank account."

I mean—**who are you? How do you feel? What kind of energy do you bring into a room?**

---

**THE PROBLEM WITH GOALS**

Most people set goals like:

- "I want to make $X"
- "I want to lose X pounds"
- "I want to get promoted"

Nothing wrong with goals. But here's what they miss:

**External goals don't change who you are. They change what you HAVE.**

And if you don't like who you're being right now, having more of everything won't fix it.

---

**TODAY'S EXERCISE: Your Vision Statement**

Write a vision for your future self that's about **who you're becoming**, not just what you're achieving.

Use these prompts:

1. "Imagine yourself 3 years from now. You wake up in the morning. How do you feel before you even get out of bed?"

2. "You walk into a room. What energy do people sense from you?"

3. "How do you treat yourself when things go wrong?"

4. "What have you stopped tolerating?"

5. "What kind of friend, partner, parent, or leader have you become?"

6. "What does a 'good day' look like—not because everything went perfectly, but because of how you handled it?"

Write it in present tense. Like it's already true.

"I am someone who..."

"I wake up feeling..."

"People around me experience me as..."

---

**THIS MATTERS**

When you have a clear vision of who you're becoming, every decision gets easier.

Should I take this job? Does it align with who I'm becoming?

Should I stay in this relationship? Does it align with who I'm becoming?

Should I say yes to this commitment? Does it align with who I'm becoming?
